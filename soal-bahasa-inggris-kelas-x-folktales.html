22. The characteristics of folktales are as follow, except. ​.


Jawaban:

The characteristics of folktales are as follows:

1. Oral tradition: Folktales are passed down orally from generation to generation and are not written down until much later.

2. Anonymous authorship: Folktales do not have a single, identifiable author, as they are the product of a collective culture.

3. Collective ownership: Folktales belong to the community or culture that created them, rather than to an individual.

4. Traditional structure: Folktales often follow a traditional structure or pattern, such as the "once upon a time" opening and the "happily ever after" ending.

5. Moral or lesson: Folktales often contain a moral or lesson that teaches values or lessons about life.

6. Supernatural elements: Folktales often include supernatural elements, such as magic, mythical creatures, and enchanted objects.

The characteristic that does not belong on this list is "Historical accuracy". Folktales are not necessarily based on historical events, and are not meant to be a record of history.

Jawaban: An Old Man in the Village

An old man lived in the village. He was one of the most unfortunate people in the world. The whole village was tired of him; he was always gloomy, he constantly complained and was always in a bad mood.

The longer he lived, the more bile he was becoming and the more poisonous were his words. People avoided him because his misfortune became contagious. It was even unnatural and insulting to be happy next to him.

He created the feeling of unhappiness in others.

But one day, when he turned eighty years old, an incredible thing happened. Instantly everyone started hearing the rumour that the Old Man had become happy, he doesn’t complain about anything anymore, always smiles, and even his face is freshened up.

The whole village gathered together. The villagers asked the old man about what happened.

He answers, “Nothing special. Eighty years I’ve been chasing happiness, and it was useless. And then I decided to live without happiness and just enjoy life. That’s why I’m happy now.”

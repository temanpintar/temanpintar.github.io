31. What are the different forms of questions found in language, such as yes-no questions, wh-questions, tag questions, and rhetorical questions? How does each form serve a distinct purpose in communication?


Jawaban:

1. Yes-No Questions:

  - Purpose: These questions are like asking if something is true or not. For example, "Is it raining?" You can answer with "yes" or "no."

2. Wh-Questions:

  - Purpose: These questions are for getting more information. They usually start with words like who, what, where, when, why, or how. For example, "What's your favorite color?" You need to give more than just "yes" or "no."

3. Tag Questions:

  - Purpose: These are like asking someone to agree with you. They come at the end of a sentence, like saying, "It's a nice day, isn't it?" You expect the other person to agree.

4. Rhetorical Questions:

  - Purpose: These are questions you ask, but you don't really expect an answer. You use them to make a point, like saying, "Who wouldn't want ice cream?" You're not really asking for an answer; you're just saying that everyone likes ice cream.

These different questions help us talk to each other, learn things, and share our thoughts and ideas in different ways. It's like having different tools for different jobs when we talk to people.

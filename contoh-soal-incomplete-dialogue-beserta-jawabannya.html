14. fungsi obligations dalam kehidupan sehari ~hari ??? dan berikan contoh dialogue obligation disertai alur cerita dialong ny ?


Yang dimaksud dengan obligations dalam bahasa Inggris adalah kewajiban. Dalam percakapan sehari-hari, terkadang muncul kalimat-kalimat yang mendiskusikan tentang kewajiban, baik dalam lingkup sosial keluarga, di sekolah, maupun di lingkungan kerja atau lingkup sosial lainnya.

Untuk lingkup sosial keluarga, kewajiban yang dimaksudkan bisa menyangkut pekerjaan rumah tangga, kewajiban orang tua, kewajiban anak-anak atau kemungkinan situasi lainnya. Sedangkan di lingkup sosial sekolah, kewajiban bisa meliputi disiplin waktu, keharusan berseragam, berbahasa yang baik dan benar, menaati peraturan sekolah dan lain-lain.

Berikut contoh-contoh percakapan yang berhubungan dengan obligations:

1. Di rumah (percakapan keluarga)

Mother: Tommy, have you tidied up your bedroom? It's been 2 weeks since the last time you did it, and you know you have do it yourself now that you're already in 10th grade.Tommy: Okay, mom, but I cannot do it now because I have a lot of homework to do.Mother: You're not lying to me, are you? What homework do you have?Tommy: I swear I'm not lying, mom. I have physics and history homework. The physics homework is due tomorrow, and the history paper the following day.Mother: Don't you have a physics class this afternoon? See if you can get some help from your tutor. Anyway, I expect to see your room spick and span tomorrow.Tommy: I promise, mom.

*Alur dialog: Tommy wajib membersihkan kamarnya dari waktu ke waktu karena umurnya sekarang sudah cukup besar untuk mulai bertanggung jawab  atas pekerjaan rumah tertentu seperti membersihkan kamarnya sendiri.

2. Di sekolah (antara guru dan murid)

Mr. Tono: Budi, I have not received your paper. Did you submit it this morning?Budi: No, Sir. I submitted it 2 days ago. Didn't you say the due date was yesterday?Mr. Tono: I did, but due to the school anniversary event, I have given the students a few more days to complete it. Did you say you submitted it 2 days ago? Whom did you give it to?Budi: I went to the teacher's office and put it on your desk, Sir. I put it on the yellow folder.Mr. Tono: Are you sure it was my desk? I don't have any yellow folder Budi. You should go to the office now, find your paper, and give it to me. In the future, you have to make sure I receive your work in my hands.Budi: I will, Sir. Thank you, Sir.

*Alur dialog: Budi mengumpulkan tugas tanpa menyerahkan langsung ke gurunya dan malah meletakkan tugasnya di meja guru yang salah. Pak Tono mengingatkan agar di kali berikutnya, dia memastikan tugasnya diterima langsung oleh Pak Tononya sendiri.

3. Di kantor perusahaan

Mr. Lee (Manager): Ms. Green, can you come to my office now?Ms. Green (Secretary): Yes, Mr. Lee. Would you like me to bring you anything?Mr. L: Nothing for now. And lock the door on your way in.(In Mr. Lee's office)Mr. Lee: Ms. Green, I would like you to supervise our marketing team. There is a rumor of information leak about our new marketing project, and I suspect the marketing team is aware of this problem but do not report it despite their obligation to do so.Ms. Green: How would you like to handle this, Sir?Mr. Lee: You will work closely with them and tell them that you're a new addition to the team to make sure the marketing team meet their target before deadline. You shall report directly to me and Mr. Latham from Internal Affairs. I hope you understand the severity of this problem.Ms. Green: Understood, Sir. I won't disappoint you, Sir.Mr. Lee: That would be all, Ms. Green.

*Alur dialog: Nona Green diberi tugas mengawasi tim pemasaran karena ada isu pembocoran info perusahaan, dan Tuan Lee menduga tim tersebut mengetahui akan hal ini tapi tidak melaporkannya padahal sudah menjadi kewajiban mereka untuk melaporkannya. Nona Green ditugaskan langsung atas arahan Tuan Lee dan Tuan Latham untuk memata-matai pekerjaan tim pemasarannya langsung dengan membaur dengan mereka.

16. incomplete adjective clauses


Jawaban:

An adjective clause—also called an adjectival or relative clause—will meet three requirements:

First, it will contain a subject and verb.

Next, it will begin with a relative pronoun [who, whom, whose, that, or which] or a relative adverb [when, where, or why].

Finally, it will function as an adjective, answering the questions What kind? How many? or Which one?
